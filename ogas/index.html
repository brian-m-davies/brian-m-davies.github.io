Oga’s Cantina

Behind The Music

Introduction

If you're fascinated by who on Earth actually creates songs in the voices of jawas and droids, this is a compendium of information on the artists who created the music that plays in Oga's Cantina in Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge.

Two of the producers of the music for Oga's Cantina were Matt Walker, the executive in charge of music at Walt Disney Imagineering, and Yaron Spiwak, a senior creative director at Walt Disney Imagineering. The pair were interviewed by Billboard about the general strategy for the project.

They credit LucasFilm employees Matt Wood from Skywalker Sound and David Collins for writing the lyrics in the alien languages of Star Wars. Wood is a 5-time Academy Award nominee for sound editing, and also the voice of General Grievous and a few other characters. Collins is the lead sound designer / voice director with the video game division of LucasArts and has done some acting and voice acting for them as well.

Matt Walker also hosted an hour-long panel discussion at D23 Expo 2019 about the music, featuring singer Judith Hill, Disney Group songwriter Jason Mater, Matt Wood, and composer / producer Adam Dorn.

The Songs

6 tracks were released as a preview on August 23, 2019, the day before the D23 Expo, and then the full "R3X's Playlist #1" with 18 tracks was released as a digital download on September 6, 2019. The playlist is available for purchase, and is also available on YouTube for the price of watching a few ads.

"The Sims 4 Star Wars: Journey To Batuu" includes two radio stations, "DJ R-3X Stereo", and "Batuu Radio". The former includes 2 songs, Blue Milk Surprise and Til The Spire, which are almost certainly used in Oga's Cantina. The latter includes 6 songs, 3 of which are probably used in Oga's Cantina. (The other 3 from "Batuu Radio" are not used in Oga's Cantina, and are covered later, in the section on ambient music.)

  1. Blue Milk Surprise by Adam Gubman and Matthew Wood
  2. Til The Spire by Tamir Muskat and Ori Kaplan
  3. Galma by Guy Mayzig
  4. Victory on Ryloth by Kevin Kiner
  5. Ti Lo Laka by Beckerman Green & Douek

In addition, 3 more unreleased tracks, "R3X Opening Track", "Doolstan", and "Gaya" are available on the Brewhouse Music site.

According to an L.A. Times article, 29 songs were commissioned. The 18 songs from the playlist, the 5 songs from The Sims, and the 3 songs from Brewhouse Music make 26, so there are likely a handful of Oga's Cantina songs still unreleased.

The Artists

A track by track look at the people who created the music...

1. Mad About Mad About Me

R3X, featuring Figrin D’an and the Modal Nodes

This is a dance remix of the iconic 1977 John Williams song, known simply as "Cantina Band" on the original Star Wars soundtrack. It later became known as "Mad About Me" by Figrin D’an and the Modal Nodes.

The purported remixer is R3X, the robot DJ, formerly (originally RX-24 aka "REX") who piloted the Star Tours ride. He is voiced by Paul Reubens, better known by his stage name Pee Wee Herman. The track info does not credit the actual human who did the remix, but this article credits producer Adam Dorn.

Mus Kat & Nalpak

B. Til The Spire

Mus Kat & Nalpak is a play on the last names of the composers, percussionist Tamir Muskat and saxophonist Ori Kaplan. Both grew up in Israel and now work in Brooklyn. The duo founded Balkan Beat Box, a world music fusion band with heavy klezmer and punk influences. What they do doesn’t take much alteration to fit in Batuu. If you want more music like that in Oga’s Cantina, definitely check out Balkan Beat Box.

There is a fourth Mus Kat and Nalpak song, Til The Spire, in "The Sims 4 Star Wars: Journey To Batuu".

For More: Balkan Beat Box has a YouTube channel with many of their songs; a good place to start is Kum Kum.

4. Nama Heh

Laki Lembeng

Created by Francis Ralls, who made his name doing TV show music, including Dancing With The Stars and NBC’s NFL broadcasts. He has done other projects for Disney, including the music for the Color Fest Street Parade at Disneyland Shanghai.

For More: Ralls has released an EP called Piano Take 1, and created a number of soundtracks; the soundtrack for the movie The Ride has been released. Samples of both are available on YouTube. His name is also on a 2018 version of Joy To The World, one of the tracks on an album called Oh Christmas Glee. Of more interest to Star Wars fans are the 5 tracks he contributed to a collection called Space Adventure 2 in 2018. One of them is available on YouTube.

5. Oola Shuka

Gaya

This is by Judith Hill. She started her career as a backing vocalist, supporting artists like Michael Jackson, Rod Stewart, Carole King, Stevie Wonder, and Gregg Allman. In 2013, she won a Grammy for Best Music Film for her part in 20 Feet From Stardom, a documentary about backup singers.

For More: Hill released her debut solo album in 2015, produced by Prince, and has since put out two more albums. Much of that material is available on her YouTube channel.

6. Beep Boop Bop

Duro Droids

Composed by Melissa Atwell, a Disney regular. She has worked on projects for every Disney Park, and has also done work for AT&T, Lockheed Martin, the Orlando Magic, Sesame Street, and Six Flags.

7. Aloogahoo

Vee Gooda & Ryco

The band name is a play on the names of the composers, Valerie Vigoda and Ryan O’Connell.

Vigoda is a singer, electric violin player, and composer. She is a founding member of the musical group GrooveLily, and has toured with Cyndy Lauper and Joe Jackson. She has written a number of musicals with usual collaborator and now husband Brendan Milburn.

For More: The two collaborated on Ernest Shackleton Loves Me, a stage play that was turned into a movie, where Vigoda starred, and O’Connell was music director. A few songs from that, plus a lot of other material by Vigoda, are on her YouTube channel.

8. Bai Tee Tee

Junee Veech, featuring Gubz

A. Blue Milk Surprise

Gubz is Adam Gubman. He is best known as a composer for video games, having scored over 600 titles since Pirates Of The Burning Sea in 2006. He has also done a good number of commercials, a few movies, and he is a regular composer for Disney Park events. He has worked with Michael Bolton, Carole King, and Spinal Tap. His company is Moonwalk Audio.

Gubman and Skywalker Sound voice actor Matthew Wood are behind the track Blue Milk Surprise in "The Sims 4: Journey To Batuu".

For More: Gubman also produced the Grammy winning and Oscar nominated song This Is Me, from The Greatest Showman. More recently he created Seeds, an animated musical. His music for Arknights is excellent.

9. Modal Notes

Figrin D’an and the Modal Nodes

10. Una Duey Dee

Kra Mer 5

Both songs are by Michael Kramer. The former is done in the style of the original cantina band, so is “by” Figrin D’an. The latter band is a play on his last name. Kramer was inspired to become a composer by Jurassic Park, and went on to score Lego Star Wars, so is deeply familiar with John Williams’ work. GWW did a great interview with him, about his background, his inspirations, and his process.

For More: Kramer's YouTube channel mostly contains samples from his soundtrack to the Lego TV series Ninjago.

12. Droid World

Sentient 7 and the Clankers

The first track is Zain Effendi and Tyler Martens, the second is Zain Effendi and Dylan Lane. Effendi worked on the team that did the music for Pirates Of The Caribbean 3 and a number of other high-profile movies, then went solo. He is represented by Air Edel.

For More: Check out Effendi's song Dark Mission.

13. Utinni

The Dusty Jawas

Yossi Cohen and Avishay Goren are two of the composers at Brewhouse Music. They are primarily composers of commercials. The pair did “Movement is Happiness (Find Your Thing)” for Just Dance 2015. A bio of Goren is available on Sync Summit.

For More: The Brewhouse Music site includes a portfolio, which has 3 unreleased Oga's Cantina tracks.

(There are two musicians out there named Yossi Cohen; previous versions of this pointed to the wrong Yossi Cohen, the Brooklyn-based Lubavitcher keyboardist and singer. Who is also fantastic.)

14. Batuu Boogie

Chaka Mater Laka, featuring MW-59

Jason Mater is another Disney regular. He is a producer and songwriter. The band name is his name spliced into the sounds in the song. He was listed on the Disney Music Publishing site previously, but is not currently.

For More: Mater recently cowrote a country hit with Sam Grow called Nobody’s You. He has a long list of credits on AllMusic.

C. Galma

Galma from "The Sims 4 Star Wars: Journey To Batuu" was written by Guy Mayzig, an Israeli musician from Lod.

For More: Check out Mayzig's YouTube channel.

D. Victory on Ryloth

The track in "The Sims 4 Star Wars: Journey To Batuu" called Victory on Ryloth is very likely mislabeled. There is a track by that name on the Clone Wars soundtrack, written by Kevin Kiner, during the scene where they are fighting on Ryloth, which is a completely different track. We probably don't actually know the correct name or composer of this song.

E. Ti Lo Laka

Ti Lo Laka from "The Sims 4 Star Wars: Journey To Batuu" is by Beckerman Green & Douek. Beckerman is probably Joel Beckerman, since his company Made Music Studio does other branding music for Disney. Douek is probably Joel Douek.

Ambient

Three of the tracks in "The Sims 4 Star Wars: Journey To Batuu" are ambient music, which plays in the streets of Galaxy's Edge, not in Oga's Cantina. The first two play in the marketplace, the third plays in the docking bay.

The tracks are credited to Randy Kerber and Harlan Hodges. Kerber and Hodges are 2 of the 3 people who comprise Aporia Entertainment.

  • Marketplace by Harlan Hodges
  • Marketplace Hora by Randy Kerber
  • Mischievous by Randy Kerber

In addition, short (under 1 minute) excerpts of Marketplace and 6 other ambient pieces are available on Harlan Hodges' site.

Diagetic

R3X's Playlist includes a remix of Cantina Band, the most recognizable of John Williams’ compositions for the Star Wars movies. But that is just one of the many pieces of diagetic music in the movies. There is plenty more where that came from. There are 4 other pieces by John Williams:

  • Cantina Band #2 by Figrin D'an, from Star Wars, which plays in the background during Han's negotiation with Luke and Obi Wan
  • Lapti Nek by Sy Snootles and The Max Rebo Band, from Return Of The Jedi, plays in Jabba's throne room
  • Galactic Dance Blast, never released on any soundtrack album, which the Max Rebo Band plays as background music on the sail barge
  • Canto Bight from the casino in The Last Jedi

(Lapti Nek was replaced in the 1997 Special Edition release of Return Of The Jedi by Jedi Rocks, written by Jerry Hey.)

For the sequel trilogy, Lin-Manuel Miranda was asked by J.J. Abrams to produce several pieces of music. The performer for all 3 of them is listed as Shag Kava.

  • Jabba Flow plays in The Force Awakens, as Han Solo, Finn, Rey, and BB-8 enter Maz Kanata's castle (Lin-Manuel Miranda has also done the song as a street performance!)
  • Dobra Doompa is also from The Force Awakens, but is not on the soundtrack; it plays later in Maz Kanata's castle
  • Lido Hey is the song The Rise Of Skywalker during the festival of the ancestors; there's no clean recording of it available
